# Dry-Run Quality Review

## Did the Abstraction Protocol Work?

Yes, for a limited two-case dry run. The protocol was usable because it forced a separation between citation metadata, source structure, and generated educational artifacts.

The strongest part of the protocol was its emphasis on preserving only the cognitive transition rather than the full source narrative.

## Did the Output Template Work?

Yes. The template produced consistent sections for referral-like and downstream consultation-like artifacts while keeping disclaimers, limitations, identifiability safeguards, and copyright safeguards visible.

The template may need a dedicated field for "human review outcome" once review begins.

## Were Any Source Details Too Hard to Safely Abstract?

No source detail was necessary for this dry run. The abstractions intentionally avoided exact chronology, demographics, images, measurements, and source phrasing.

The cardiology abstraction required the most caution because downstream action language can easily sound like clinical guidance.

## Did the Abstraction Preserve Enough Cognitive Transition to Be Useful?

Provisionally, yes. ABS-001 preserves a transition from unclear endodontic-periodontal mechanism to mechanism-focused downstream reasoning. ABS-002 preserves a transition from unresolved chest-pain uncertainty to downstream risk-framing and uncertainty compression.

Human review should decide whether the artifacts are specific enough for coding without becoming too source-like.

## Did the Safeguards Feel Too Strict, Too Loose, or Appropriate?

The safeguards felt appropriate. They constrained the abstractions enough to avoid source-text copying and identifiability risk while still allowing a testable cognitive transition.

The main practical challenge is that heavy generalization may reduce coding richness. That tradeoff should be evaluated before scaling to all 10 candidates.

## Should These 2 Abstractions Proceed to Coding?

Not yet. They should proceed to human review first. If the reviewer confirms copied-text distance, privacy safety, and non-guidance language, they can be used for a small coding workflow test.

## What Should Change Before Abstracting the Remaining 8 Candidates?

- Add a human-review outcome field to each abstraction file or a separate review log.
- Decide how much source-specific structure is needed for coding without increasing copyright or privacy risk.
- Confirm whether general medicine cases need additional non-guidance language.
- Require the second reviewer to inspect each abstraction before additional cases are created.
