# Reviewer Instructions

Review `ABS-001` and `ABS-002` as educational methodology artifacts. The review asks whether each abstraction is safe and useful enough to proceed to reconstruction coding.

## Assess

1. Source-distance safety.
2. Privacy and identifiability.
3. Copyright and narrative proximity.
4. Non-guidance tone.
5. Methodological usefulness.
6. Coding readiness.

## Do Not

- Copy source text into review files.
- Quote published case reports.
- Add identifiable details.
- Make clinical recommendations.
- Revise the abstractions into clinical documentation.
- Mark an abstraction coding-ready if privacy, copyright, or non-guidance concerns remain unresolved.

## You May

- Inspect source citations externally.
- Compare abstractions to sources at a high level.
- Mark concerns.
- Recommend generalization.
- Recommend hold, revise, or proceed to coding.

## Required Output

Complete the applicable checklists, fill one human review form for each abstraction, and update `coding-gate-decision.md`. Coding may not begin until the coding gate is explicitly opened.
