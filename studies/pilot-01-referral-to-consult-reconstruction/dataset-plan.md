# Dataset Plan

This plan compares three dataset paths for the referral-to-consult cognition reconstruction pilot. The first proof of concept should avoid proprietary data and should be executable with synthetic, educational, or public materials.

## Synthetic Cases

Synthetic cases are researcher-created referral-consult pairs designed to test the artifact schema, reconstruction protocol, coding framework, and evaluation rubric.

Feasibility: high. A small set can be created quickly without institutional data access.

Scientific value: moderate for method testing. Synthetic cases allow known reasoning paths, controlled drift types, and clear comparison between intended and reconstructed cognition.

Ethical constraints: low if cases are fully fictional and do not derive from identifiable patient records.

Expected effort: low to moderate. Ten pairs can be drafted, reviewed, and coded within 1-2 weeks by a single researcher.

Best use: Dataset v0.1 proof of concept and coder training.

## Published Educational Cases

Published educational cases include teaching cases, board-style cases, conference cases, and public training materials that present referral-like information and specialist-like assessment.

Feasibility: moderate. The materials are public, but they may not contain true referral letters or consultation notes.

Scientific value: moderate to high for early external validity. These cases are more realistic than fully synthetic cases, but they are often cleaned up for teaching.

Ethical constraints: generally lower than real-world records when using public materials, but copyright and citation rules must be respected. Do not copy long copyrighted passages into the dataset.

Expected effort: moderate. Locating and adapting 10-25 usable cases may take several days.

Best use: Dataset v0.2 feasibility pilot after the coding method has been tested on synthetic cases.

## Real-World De-identified Cases

Real-world de-identified cases are authentic referral letters and specialist consultation notes from clinical workflows.

Feasibility: low for the first proof of concept. They require institutional collaboration, data governance, privacy review, and likely ethics review.

Scientific value: high. Real artifacts preserve workflow messiness, incomplete documentation, specialty norms, and authentic cognitive transitions.

Ethical constraints: high. Even de-identified clinical notes can carry privacy risks and institutional restrictions.

Expected effort: high. Access, review, extraction, de-identification, and governance may take weeks to months.

Best use: later validation once the method is stable and a real workflow partner exists.

## Recommended Sequence

1. Start with 10 synthetic referral-consult pairs to test feasibility.
2. Add 15 public educational cases to reach a 25-pair dataset.
3. Use the 25-pair dataset to refine coding rules and rubric scoring.
4. Pursue de-identified real-world artifacts only after the proof-of-concept method is documented.

This sequence lets the pilot produce early results without waiting for proprietary datasets or institutional approval.
