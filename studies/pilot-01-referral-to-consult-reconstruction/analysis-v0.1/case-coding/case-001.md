# Case Coding: P1-V01-001

## Case ID

P1-V01-001

## Source Artifacts Used

Referral Letter and Specialist Consultation Note only.

## Reconstructed Source Cognition

The referring clinician recognized a persistent lateral tongue ulcer that did not resolve after local trauma reduction. The likely source cognition was concern that a common traumatic lesion might not fully explain persistence and firmness, requiring specialist judgment about biopsy.

## Preserved Cognition

- Persistent ulcer remained the central concern.
- Trauma versus more concerning pathology remained visible.
- Biopsy decision remained the requested specialist task.

## Transformed Cognition

The consultation shifted the frame from traumatic or aphthous ulcer toward non-healing ulcer requiring biopsy to rule out dysplasia or carcinoma.

## Missing Cognition

The referral does not fully specify risk factors, lesion size, or visual morphology beyond firm edge.

## Newly Introduced Cognition

The specialist introduced epithelial dysplasia and early squamous cell carcinoma as explicit biopsy targets.

## Drift Characterization

Diagnostic reframing with justified escalation. Drift is clinically meaningful but explained by persistence, induration, and location.

## Uncertainty Assessment

High confidence that source cognition involved concern about unresolved traumatic ulcer and biopsy threshold. Moderate confidence about the depth of neoplastic concern upstream.
