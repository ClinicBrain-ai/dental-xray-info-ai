# Case Coding: P1-V01-010

## Case ID

P1-V01-010

## Source Artifacts Used

Referral Letter and Specialist Consultation Note only.

## Reconstructed Source Cognition

The referring clinician recognized new iron deficiency anemia and uncertainty between benign dietary deficiency and occult gastrointestinal blood loss. Referral sought specialist threshold-setting for endoscopic evaluation.

## Preserved Cognition

- Iron deficiency anemia remained the central clinical problem.
- Occult GI blood loss versus dietary deficiency remained visible.
- Need for endoscopy decision remained the consultation request.

## Transformed Cognition

The specialist reframed evaluation toward GI workup as necessary rather than optional, while preserving dietary deficiency as a less secure contributor.

## Missing Cognition

Referral lacks medication history, menstrual or dietary details, stool test result, and cancer-risk context.

## Newly Introduced Cognition

Colonic neoplasm, upper GI bleeding source, malabsorption, medication-associated bleeding, and bidirectional endoscopy were introduced.

## Drift Characterization

Uncertainty compression and mechanism prioritization toward occult GI pathology.

## Uncertainty Assessment

High confidence. The source uncertainty and treatment intent are explicit; details behind benign explanations remain incomplete.
