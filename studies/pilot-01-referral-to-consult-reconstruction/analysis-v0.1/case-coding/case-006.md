# Case Coding: P1-V01-006

## Case ID

P1-V01-006

## Source Artifacts Used

Referral Letter and Specialist Consultation Note only.

## Reconstructed Source Cognition

The referring clinician initially considered candidiasis but recognized that normal mucosa and failed empiric antifungal treatment made that explanation weak. Referral sought oral medicine evaluation for persistent burning symptoms.

## Preserved Cognition

- Burning tongue and palate symptoms remained central.
- Candidiasis and burning mouth syndrome remained in the differential.
- Need to explain symptoms despite normal mucosa remained visible.

## Transformed Cognition

The specialist reframed the case toward probable primary burning mouth syndrome after excluding visible mucosal disease.

## Missing Cognition

Referral does not include medication list, nutritional screening, salivary flow assessment, or systemic review.

## Newly Introduced Cognition

Nutritional deficiency, medication effect, xerostomia contributors, and neuropathic pain management were introduced.

## Drift Characterization

Diagnostic replacement and mechanism substitution from infectious/mucosal explanation toward neuropathic or idiopathic syndrome.

## Uncertainty Assessment

Moderate confidence. The initial uncertainty is explicit, but systemic contributors cannot be reconstructed from the referral.
