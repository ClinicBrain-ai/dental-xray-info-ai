# Case Coding: P1-V01-002

## Case ID

P1-V01-002

## Source Artifacts Used

Referral Letter and Specialist Consultation Note only.

## Reconstructed Source Cognition

The referring clinician was trying to distinguish severe expected post-extraction pain from alveolar osteitis or infection. Referral transferred assessment of socket healing and management decision to oral surgery.

## Preserved Cognition

- Post-extraction pain remained the core problem.
- Dry socket versus infection remained the differential.
- Management of healing socket remained the intent.

## Transformed Cognition

The consultation narrowed uncertainty toward alveolar osteitis and explained why infection was unlikely.

## Missing Cognition

The referral does not document socket appearance in detail beyond absence of visible purulence.

## Newly Introduced Cognition

The specialist introduced exposed socket bone, lymphadenopathy absence, retained root fragment, and temporomandibular referred pain as additional reasoning elements.

## Drift Characterization

Uncertainty compression from two plausible complications to a favored diagnosis. Drift is well explained by specialist findings.

## Uncertainty Assessment

High confidence that the source cognition involved dry socket versus infection. Low concern for over-inference because the referral explicitly states the uncertainty.
