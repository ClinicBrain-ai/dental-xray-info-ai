# Candidate Sources

This file identifies categories of public or educational sources that may contain material suitable for constructing referral-consult artifact pairs. It does not collect, scrape, copy, or redistribute source data.

## Published Case Reports

Published case reports may include presentation, referral history, specialist assessment, treatment plan, and follow-up. They often lack authentic referral letters, but can support reconstructed or educational artifact pairs.

Potential value:

- real clinical complexity;
- explicit assessment and rationale;
- follow-up information.

Expected limitation:

- retrospectively written and polished;
- may not preserve original referral cognition.

## Educational Conferences

Educational conferences, morbidity and mortality conferences, grand rounds, and case discussion formats may present staged clinical reasoning with specialist commentary.

Potential value:

- explicit reasoning;
- rich teaching structure;
- discussion of uncertainty and alternatives.

Expected limitation:

- often designed for pedagogy rather than authentic workflow reconstruction.

## Specialty Training Materials

Residency, fellowship, board review, and continuing education materials may contain referral-like prompts and specialist-style assessments.

Potential value:

- domain-specific reasoning;
- concise case formats;
- available across specialties.

Expected limitation:

- may simplify uncertainty and omit documentation artifacts.

## Textbook Cases

Textbook cases can provide structured presentations, differential diagnoses, management decisions, and explanatory rationale.

Potential value:

- clear clinical logic;
- useful for synthetic pair construction;
- stable examples for coder training.

Expected limitation:

- copyrighted;
- may be too idealized for workflow analysis.

## Public Clinical Teaching Repositories

Some universities, professional societies, and open education projects publish case-based teaching resources.

Potential value:

- accessible without clinical data agreements;
- useful for early proof-of-concept examples.

Expected limitation:

- variable permissions;
- inconsistent artifact structure.

## Simulated Cases Created by the Study Team

Fully synthetic cases can be designed to include known transformations such as uncertainty loss, reframing, or mechanism substitution.

Potential value:

- no patient privacy issue;
- controlled reasoning path;
- useful for testing the coding framework.

Expected limitation:

- limited external validity;
- may reflect researcher assumptions.

## Source Selection Rule

For Dataset v0.1, prioritize sources that can be used without scraping, proprietary access, or patient-level institutional data. When using copyrighted educational material, record bibliographic references and abstract the case structure rather than copying protected text.
