## LinkedIn Long Version

I have been gradually reframing an earlier dental AI infrastructure project into a broader research direction: the Clinical Cognition Transformation Lab (CCTL).

The project began with practical infrastructure questions. How could a dental case be packaged for AI use? What clinical context should be represented? Could something like a Dental Context Specification help preserve the information needed for downstream interpretation?

Those questions remained useful, but they also revealed a larger problem. The central issue was not only whether clinical data could be made AI-ready. It was how clinical cognition itself changes as it moves through clinicians, AI systems, documentation artifacts, workflows, institutions, patients, and caregivers.

CCTL studies Human-AI Clinical Cognition: the distributed cognitive processes that emerge when clinical decisions are jointly shaped by humans, machines, documents, organizations, and patients. The focus is not only AI performance or automation. It is cognition transformation: preservation, compression, delegation, reconstruction, reframing, provenance, and human-AI co-cognition.

The repository now includes a manifesto, research agenda, early paper drafts, pilot study materials, Research Note 001, and a closed Dataset v0.2 methodology cycle. Dataset v0.2 was exploratory and educational. It used bounded abstraction and coding workflows with human review gates, and it is now closed. Research Note 002 remains prohibited.

I have also published Status Report v1.0, which is the best entry point for understanding the current repository status, completed work, active boundaries, and open methodological questions.

This work is research-oriented and methodology-focused. It does not claim clinical validation, diagnostic accuracy, clinical effectiveness, treatment quality, patient outcomes, or deployment readiness. It does not provide clinical guidance, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or patient-care advice.
