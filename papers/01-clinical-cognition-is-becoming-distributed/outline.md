# Clinical Cognition Is Becoming Distributed: Outline

## Working Position

Clinical cognition is becoming distributed across clinicians, AI systems, documentation artifacts, institutional workflows, healthcare organizations, patients, and caregivers. The paper should define this shift without overstating the maturity, benefits, or risks of current AI systems.

## Intended Article Type

Perspective or Viewpoint article.

## Core Claim

The primary unit of analysis should expand from individual clinical reasoning to Human-AI Clinical Cognition: the distributed cognitive process by which clinical interpretation, recommendation, explanation, and action are produced across human and artificial participants.

## Section Logic

1. Abstract
   - State the shift from individual reasoning to distributed clinical cognition.
   - Introduce AI as a participant in cognition, not merely a tool.
   - Position CCTL as a research program.

2. Introduction
   - Describe AI-mediated clinical work.
   - Explain why performance metrics alone are insufficient.
   - Introduce transformation as the central concern.

3. From Individual Reasoning to Distributed Clinical Cognition
   - Respect the importance of individual reasoning.
   - Show why clinical meaning now moves through artifacts and systems.
   - Define distributed clinical cognition as a broader analytic frame.

4. Why AI Changes the Unit of Analysis
   - Explain how AI changes attention, salience, documentation, recommendation, and reconstruction.
   - Avoid claiming AI has human judgment or moral agency.
   - Focus on cognitive participation and pathway effects.

5. Human-AI Clinical Cognition
   - Provide the definition.
   - Treat cognition as system-level activity.
   - Emphasize movement of claims, uncertainty, recommendations, explanations, and responsibility.

6. Forms of Clinical Cognition Transformation
   - Preservation.
   - Compression.
   - Delegation.
   - Translation.
   - Simplification.
   - Reframing.
   - Reconstruction.
   - Redistribution.
   - Reassignment.
   - Drift.

7. Implications for Medical Education
   - New expertise in supervision, interrogation, and metacognition.
   - Foundational knowledge remains important.
   - Education must address distributed cognitive pathways.

8. Implications for Documentation and Accountability
   - Documentation as cognitive interface.
   - Auditability of reasoning, uncertainty, and responsibility.
   - Need for cognitive provenance.

9. Research Agenda
   - Clinical Cognition Transformation.
   - Cognitive Reconstruction.
   - Human-AI Co-Cognition.
   - Cognitive Provenance.
   - Longitudinal Cognitive Change.

10. Conclusion
    - Restate the central thesis.
    - Emphasize cautious empirical study.
    - Close on CCTL's purpose as a research program.

## Style Constraints

- Academic and concise.
- Visionary but cautious.
- No product language.
- No claim that AI replaces clinicians.
- No claim that distributed cognition is automatically beneficial.
- Preserve CCTL as a research program, not a startup, standard, or software product.
